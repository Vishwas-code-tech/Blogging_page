Make time-tabel for studty



To make the most of your day before an exam, it's important to follow a structured timetable. Start your day early around 7:00 AM, freshen up, and have a light, healthy breakfast. Begin your first study session at 8:00 AM, focusing on the most important or difficult topic. After two hours of focused studying, take a 15-minute break to stretch and relax. From 10:15 AM to 12:15 PM, study another major topic or continue reviewing important notes. Take a longer break for lunch around 12:15 PM and give yourself time to relax. Resume studying at 1:00 PM with a 90-minute session, focusing on a third topic or revising notes. After a short break at 2:30 PM, start a practice session from 3:00 PM to 4:30 PM to test your knowledge with questions or flashcards. Follow it with a light snack and rest. In the evening, from 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM, go over any weak areas or confusing concepts. Have dinner around 6:00 PM and relax your mind. From 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM, do a quick recap of all the topics studied, using summaries or flashcards. Take time to unwind, and around 9:00 PM, do some light review if needed. Aim to sleep by 10:00 PM to stay well-rested and alert for your exam. Let me know if you'd like a version for night owls or for someone with limited time!
